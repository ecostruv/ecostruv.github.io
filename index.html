About EcoStruv
Eutrophication, a pervasive environmental problem, is characterized by excessive algal or plant growth in bodies of water due to elevated levels of nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus, often stemming from agricultural runoff. Modern synthetic fertilizers, rich in these nutrients, contribute significantly to this issue, causing Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs) with toxic cyanobacteria alongside dead zones.

Our Solution

EcoStruv addresses the root cause of algal blooms by recycling nitrogen and phosphorus-rich effluent from farms into struvite, a slow-release fertilizer. Our goal is to intercept nutrient-heavy runoff before it reaches bodies of water, simultaneously providing farmers with a method to repurpose these nutrients in an environmentally friendly way.


Innovation

Our innovation is an electrochemical filtration system that captures and recycles excess nutrients before they can cause environmental harm. At the heart of EcoStruv is a multi-chamber tank that is installed alongside tile drainage systems on farms. This allows us to collect and store the nutrient-rich water running off fields. Inside the tank, our novel technology uses magnesium electrodes and an electric current to crystallize the nitrogen and phosphorus into struvite. These pure struvite crystals accumulate at the bottom of the tank and can then be harvested by farmers as a sustainable, slow-release fertilizer. Unlike other filters that only purify water, EcoStruv transforms pollution into a usable product.
